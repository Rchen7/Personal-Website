Over my life, I have had a variety of extracurriculars, each with its own unique depth. One of my main extracurriculars is competition mathematics, and I first gained a passion for this when my 6th grade math teacher had my classmates and me participate in MOEMS. This competition made me realize that math doesn't have to include hard-to-memorize formulas or hard-to-understand concepts in order to be difficult; sometimes it is the way that it combines individually easier concepts that show its true complexity. Since then, I have engaged in numerous other competitions,such as MATHCOUNTS, the AMCs, AIME, and Purple Comet. This love of unique problems has spread to other subjects as well; I have participated in science and physics competitions including Science Olympiad and the F=ma competition.
